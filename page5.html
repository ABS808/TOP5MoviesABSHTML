"Goodfellas" is a critically acclaimed crime film directed by Martin Scorsese, released in 1990.
It is based on the true story of Henry Hill, a former mobster,
and is widely regarded as one of the greatest films in the gangster genre.
The film follows the rise and fall of Henry Hill, as he gets involved in the organized
crime world of the Italian-American Mafia.
Starting as a young boy fascinated by the glamorous lifestyle of the
mobsters in his Brooklyn neighborhood,
Henry aspires to become a part of their world.
Overall, "Goodfellas" is a compelling and gritty portrayal of the mobster lifestyle,
offering a gripping narrative that delves into the complexities of
human nature and the pursuit of power
within a criminal underworld.
